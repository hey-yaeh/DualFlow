# Ethics and privacy

DualFlow is a consent-aware dataset of recorded human social interaction. Ethical release therefore depends not only on what was captured, but on **how the data were consented, processed, transformed, documented, and distributed**.

## Ethics review

The data collection protocol, consent procedure, and release design were reviewed and approved by the **Trinity College Dublin Research Ethics Committee**.

## Modality-specific consent

Participants provided separate permissions for different release-relevant modalities, including:

- **voice**;
- **visual appearance**;
- **body motion**.

The released package follows these modality-specific consent boundaries rather than assuming a single blanket permission.

## Privacy protections in the release

### Egocentric video

Egocentric videos are distributed only in **identity-obscured form**. Facial mosaicing is applied during curation to reduce direct identity exposure while preserving interactional context.

### Audio

Audio is released in a **consent-aware** way. Depending on participant permissions, released audio may be:

- original cleaned dialogue; or
- transformed audio designed to reduce direct voice identification risk.

### Other modalities

Body motion, facial-performance parameters, eye-tracking signals, and transcripts are released only within the scope permitted by the signed consent forms and the gated access workflow.

## Residual risk

<div class="callout">
  <strong>Important limitation.</strong> DualFlow does not claim full anonymization. The release is designed to reduce direct identity exposure and re-identification risk while preserving the multimodal cues needed for social-interaction research.
</div>

This means users should avoid treating the release as if privacy risk had been eliminated. The combination of motion, voice, language, and social context can still create residual risk if used irresponsibly.

## Intended and prohibited use

DualFlow is intended for responsible research on topics such as multimodal social understanding, conversational coordination, role understanding, expressive animation, digital humans, and embodied agents.

The release is **not** intended for:

- participant identification or tracking across external sources;
- speaker identification on the participants in the dataset;
- attempts to reconstruct identity-revealing visual or vocal signals;
- any use outside the approved research-use conditions.

## Third-party model assets

Where the release provides derived parameter streams in formats compatible with external ecosystems, it does so without redistributing the third-party model assets themselves. This keeps the release usable while avoiding redistribution of assets that are controlled by upstream licenses.
