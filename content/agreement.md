# Research-use agreement summary

This page summarizes the expected conditions for approved access to the gated DualFlow release. It is meant as a **plain-language summary**, not as a substitute for any formal institutional agreement that may accompany access approval.

## 1. Permitted use

Approved users may use the gated release for **non-commercial academic research and teaching** within the scope described in their request.

## 2. No redistribution

You may not redistribute, sublicense, resell, publish, or otherwise share gated files with unauthorized third parties. If collaborators need access, they should be covered by the approved request or submit their own request.

## 3. No re-identification or participant contact

You may not attempt to identify participants, contact participants, infer real-world identities from the release, or combine the dataset with external resources for that purpose.

## 4. No biometric or speaker identification

The dataset must not be used to perform, train, or evaluate biometric identification, speaker identification, or related re-identification tasks on the participants in the release.

## 5. No reverse engineering of privacy protection

You may not attempt to reverse, weaken, or bypass privacy protections applied during curation, including:

- facial mosaicing or other identity-obscuring video transformations;
- voice transformation applied for consent-aware audio release.

## 6. Secure storage and limited access

Approved users should store the data securely and restrict access to authorized project personnel only. Participant-linked material should be treated as confidential research data.

## 7. Citation

Publications, demos, and derived academic outputs that use DualFlow should cite the dataset website and the accompanying paper once the final publication details are available.

## 8. Third-party assets and formats

Where the release includes derived exports compatible with SMPL-H, FLAME, or ARKit-style representations, users remain responsible for complying with any upstream license terms required by those ecosystems.

## 9. Changes to access conditions

Because the release is consent-aware, access conditions may need to be updated if the approved release package or participant permissions change over time. Maintainers will communicate any such changes to approved users when necessary.

## 10. No warranty

DualFlow is a research dataset provided on an **as-is** basis. The maintainers do not promise that the release is error-free, complete for every downstream use case, or suitable for any particular application.
